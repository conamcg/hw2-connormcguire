\## IT Support Ticket Triage System



\*\*Workflow:\*\* Automated triage of business system support tickets 

with stakeholder communication drafting



\*\*User:\*\* Senior Business Systems Manager supporting manufacturing operations



\*\*Input:\*\* Raw support ticket text submitted by business system users



\*\*Output:\*\* 

\- A triage classification — either:

&#x20; - ESCALATE: Requires senior manager attention (system bugs, deep 

&#x20;   knowledge issues). For escalated tickets, the system will also 

&#x20;   draft:

&#x20;   - An email to the business user acknowledging receipt and 

&#x20;     providing a status update

&#x20;   - An email to the contracted IT resource outlining the issue 

&#x20;     and expected resolution approach

&#x20; - DELEGATE: Can be resolved by contracted IT resources with no 

&#x20;   additional communication drafting required



\*\*Why automate this:\*\* The business users I support are master 

schedulers at manufacturing sites who manage access to select 

manufacturing slots for made-to-order cell therapies. These are 

time-critical, high-stakes operations where scheduling errors or 

system access issues can directly impact patient treatment timelines. 

A senior manager receives dozens of support tickets daily across 

multiple sites. Automatically routing routine requests to contracted 

IT frees up senior attention for the critical system issues that 

require deep business knowledge, while ensuring schedulers always 

receive timely updates on escalated issues affecting the tools they 

need to manage the manufacturing pipeline.
